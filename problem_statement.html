Problem Statement

🎯 Background

A multi-location coffee shop chain operating across Astoria, Hell's Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan has collected transactional sales data covering January–June. Despite generating over $698,812 in total revenue and recording 149,116 customer footfalls, management lacks actionable visibility into when, where, and what is driving performance.

The business needs a structured analytical framework and interactive dashboard to transform raw transactional data into clear, decision-ready insights — enabling smarter staffing, inventory planning, and promotional targeting.

❗ Core Problem

Despite having a functional Power BI dashboard, several critical business questions remain unanswered or under-explored. The current visualizations surface what happened, but do not explain why or prescribe what to do next.

  • No drill-down analysis linking hour-of-day trends to specific store locations.
  • Coffee (39%) and Tea (28%) dominate category sales — yet sub-category profitability is unexplored.
  • Footfall is nearly equal across all three stores, but revenue per visitor differences are not surfaced.
  • Order size distribution (Large 30%, Regular 31%) is measured but not correlated to revenue uplift.
  • Weekday vs. weekend patterns exist in the data but are not leveraged for operational planning.
Analytical Research Questions

The following questions — adapted from the project brief — frame the analysis scope:

Q1How do sales vary by day of the week and hour of the day?
Q2Are there identifiable peak times for sales activity, and do they differ by store?
Q3What is the total sales revenue for each month, and what is the growth trend?
Q4How do sales and footfall vary across the three store locations?
Q5What is the average price per order and how does it differ by product category?
Q6Which products and categories are the strongest revenue contributors?
Scope & Deliverables

📦 Expected Outputs

  • ✓ Power BI Dashboard — Filters by Month & Day, KPI cards, 6 visual panels (hourly trend, category pie, size distribution, store comparison, top 5 products, weekday orders).
  • EDA Report — Statistical summary, outlier detection, missing value treatment, and correlation analysis on the transactional dataset.
  • Insight Narrative — Written findings document answering all 6 research questions with supporting charts.
  • Recommendations — Actionable suggestions for staffing schedules, inventory prioritization, and promotional campaigns based on peak-time and product analysis.
